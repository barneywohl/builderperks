# BuilderPerks Guerrilla Launch Playbook

## Read

This is not a spam plan. The wedge is to make BuilderPerks feel like a useful builder-native discovery surface, then prove advertisers will pay for that attention.

The playbook from the closest analogs:

- Carbon/BuySellAds: developer trust, curated advertisers, respectful native placements.
- EthicalAds: one clean ad, privacy-first/contextual targeting, publisher control.
- daily.dev: live inside an existing developer routine and sell verified developer attention.
- Product Hunt: prepare assets and community before launch day; do not treat launch day as the beginning.

## Positioning

One-line pitch:

BuilderPerks shows useful devtool offers during AI wait time, when builders are already in shipping mode.

Builder ask:

Try the extension for one AI work session. Tell us if it feels useful, neutral, or annoying.

Advertiser ask:

Pay $250 for a 48-hour launch placement. We approve the card, show it to AI-heavy builders, and report clicks/claims.

## Strategy

Run three loops in parallel:

1. Builder Trust Loop
Get 10 real AI-heavy builders to test. The product only survives if they do not hate it.

2. Advertiser Revenue Loop
Get 5 devtool advertiser conversations and 1 paid/payment-ready pilot. Sell learning and intent, not guaranteed conversions.

3. Public Attention Loop
Use visible build-in-public updates and transparent numbers to make the surface feel alive.

## Demand Source Plan

Direct buys are the core.

External ad infra is backfill and validation, not the destination. Use developer-native networks only if they respect the UX and support the category.

Auction comes later. Launching an auction before scarcity makes the marketplace look empty. Start fixed-price. Add auction only when at least 5 advertisers repeatedly want the same audience/window.

Priority order:

1. Paid direct placements.
2. Approved house/partner offers.
3. Developer-network backfill if available.
4. Empty state asking builders for preferred perks.

## 72-Hour Guerrilla Sprint

### Hour 0-6: Proof Assets

- Record a 20-30 second screen capture: Claude/ChatGPT wait state, BuilderPerks card appears, claim recorded.
- Create one screenshot for builders and one screenshot for advertisers.
- Publish a simple live scoreboard:
  - builder testers
  - placements submitted
  - clicks
  - claims
  - feedback rating
- Prepare a public changelog post: "Built this today because AI wait time is weirdly valuable."

### Hour 6-24: Builder Seeding

Target people already posting about:

- Claude Code
- ChatGPT coding workflows
- Cursor
- v0
- Lovable
- Bolt
- Replit Agent
- AI side projects

Tactic:

- Reply publicly only when the post is relevant.
- DM only after a relevant interaction or when their profile clearly invites product/testing conversations.
- Ask for one work session, not a commitment.
- Track every response in the scoreboard.

Builder reply template:

I’m testing a tiny Chrome extension for AI builders. It shows useful devtool perks during Claude/ChatGPT wait time instead of dead spinner space.

Could you try it for one work session and tell me if it feels useful, neutral, or annoying?

Live beta: https://builderperks.netlify.app/

### Hour 12-36: Advertiser Seeding

Target companies with founder-led growth or self-serve credits:

- Railway
- Render
- Fly.io
- Neon
- Supabase
- Turso
- Langfuse
- Helicone
- Browserbase
- Firecrawl
- PostHog
- Sentry
- Checkly
- CodeRabbit
- Together
- Replicate

Tactic:

- Start with founder/growth/devrel people, not generic contact forms.
- Lead with the exact surface and the 48-hour pilot.
- Offer to write their first placement card for them.
- Close on "payment-ready" if Stripe is not fully live yet.

Advertiser DM:

We built BuilderPerks: sponsored devtool offers during Claude/ChatGPT wait states, when AI builders are actively shipping.

Launch pilot is simple: $250 for 48 hours, approval before anything goes live, and we report clicks/claims.

Want me to mock up a placement for your offer?

### Hour 24-48: Public Attention

Post the build narrative:

- "AI wait time is becoming media inventory."
- "Would you rather stare at a spinner or see useful credits/tools?"
- "We are testing whether builders tolerate sponsored perks if they are relevant and removable."
- "First 10 testers decide whether this lives or dies."

Post formats:

- Screenshot with the card in context.
- Short screen recording.
- Poll: useful / neutral / annoying.
- Transparent scoreboard update.
- Before/after: dead wait state versus useful offer.

### Hour 48-72: Conversion Push

- Publish early numbers even if small.
- Ask every builder tester for one advertiser intro.
- Ask every advertiser who says "interesting" for a written yes/no on $250.
- Convert the best 3 offers into live approved cards.
- Prepare Product Hunt only after the product has visible proof and feedback.

## Channel Tactics

### X/Twitter

Use replies as distribution. Search for people complaining about slow AI coding responses, Cursor/Claude workflows, or tool credits.

Do:

- helpful replies
- tiny demo GIF
- direct "want to test?" ask

Do not:

- mass-tag founders
- fake waitlists
- reply with irrelevant links

### Reddit

Use research and feedback posts, not promotional posts.

Good post angles:

- "Would sponsored devtool perks during AI wait time be useful or annoying?"
- "I built a tiny extension to test if AI wait states can become useful discovery."

Avoid drive-by link drops. Ask for critique first.

### Hacker News

Only post when the demo is clean and the discussion angle is interesting.

Potential title:

Show HN: BuilderPerks - useful devtool offers during AI wait time

HN risk:

Developers will punish anything that feels like intrusive ads. Lead with control, transparency, no tracking creep, and easy uninstall.

### Product Hunt

Do not launch cold. Product Hunt should be the second wave after direct outreach creates proof.

Before launch:

- demo video
- tight tagline
- maker comment explaining the experiment
- 20-30 people who have actually tested it
- clear ask for feedback, not just upvotes

## Trust Rules

- One unit per wait state.
- Clearly labeled BuilderPerks/sponsored.
- Easy disable/uninstall.
- No sensitive browsing history targeting.
- No gambling, adult, malware, fake credits, or predatory offers.
- Manual approval until trust and fraud systems exist.
- Publicly ask builders what categories should be banned.

## Metrics

North-star for week one:

- 25 builder testers
- 10 written feedback responses
- 10 advertiser conversations
- 3 payment-ready advertisers
- 1 paid pilot

Quality gates:

- Less than 20% of testers call it annoying.
- At least 1 in 10 testers clicks or claims an offer.
- At least 1 in 5 advertiser conversations says $250 is reasonable.

## Daily Operating Loop

Morning:

- Ship one product improvement.
- Send 20 builder messages/replies.
- Send 10 advertiser messages.

Afternoon:

- Review claims/clicks/feedback.
- Improve copy and placement quality.
- Post one public progress update.

Evening:

- Follow up with all replies.
- Publish scoreboard.
- Decide tomorrow’s experiment.
